## Company overview
"Founded in 1927, KENT WORLDWIDE is a family-owned company built around ag-based ingredients and consumer brands." Strength in marketing and manufacturing globally.

## Product reach
"Virtually every person across the U.S. has either used, consumed or benefited from a KENT WORLDWIDE product at one time or another." Products serve livestock, pet care, food service, retail, healthcare, and beverages.

## Vision
"Our vision is to become a recognized leader in creating value through nutritious foods and ingredients for the whole family." Frames this against global population growth and the need for innovative technology.

## Mission
"KENT WORLDWIDE exists to create lasting value for our shareholders, customers, and the communities we serve." Emphasizes strategic investment and core values.

## History timeline (1927–2023)
- **1927** — Kent 34 Baby Beef introduced in Indianola, Iowa
- **1936** — Operations relocated to Muscatine, Iowa
- **1943** — Grain Processing Corporation formed for WWII synthetic rubber program
- **1952** — Muscatine feed plant constructed
- **1953** — G.A. Kent and S.G. Stein purchase GPC facility
- **1988** — Blue Seal Feeds acquisition (founded 1868)
- **1992** — Precision Pack Foods and Frostline acquisitions
- **1995** — Dacus Foods acquisition
- **1997** — World's Best Cat Litter development
- **1999** — Washington, Indiana wet milling facility opens
- **2005** — Renamed Muscatine Foods Corporation
- **2010** — KENT Pet Group and KENT Nutrition Group formed
- **2012** — Rebranded as KENT Corporation
- **2015** — $83 million dryer house completed
- **2018** — FibreCycle Pty Ltd acquisition (Australia)
- **2020** — $37 million Sheldon, Iowa feed mill opens
- **2021** — State-of-the-art distillery launch; PKLfreeze product introduction; President's "E" Award for Exports
- **2022** — $4 million community health clinic commitment; Lovitts and Our Bird acquisitions; Natural Products, Inc. acquisition
- **2023** — World's Best Cat Litter plant expansion; McKU Plaza renovation; solar agreement; $8 million Iowa State University gift; specialty flour facility acquisition; New England retail acquisitions; Frosty Boy Global acquisition
